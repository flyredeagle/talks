# C) Lecture notes (Narrative + derivations)

## C1) Sakurai track — measurement and eigenstates

### Ideal measurement

An **ideal measurement** of observable $$A$$:

*   has possible outcomes $$a$$
*   leaves the system in a state compatible with that outcome

Empirically:

*   repeat measurement → same result
*   thus post-measurement state must be an **eigenstate**

***

## C2) Dirac track — eigenvalue equation

### Definition

A ket $$|a\rangle$$ is an eigenstate of $$\hat A$$ with eigenvalue $$a$$ if:

$$
\hat A|a\rangle = a|a\rangle
$$

Matrix form:

$$
A\mathbf v = a\mathbf v
$$

***

## C3) Degeneracy

### Definition

An eigenvalue $$a$$ is **degenerate** if there are multiple linearly independent eigenstates:

$$
\hat A|a,\alpha\rangle = a|a,\alpha\rangle,\quad \alpha=1,\dots,g
$$

*   The eigenspace is a **subspace**
*   Measurement outcome tells you $$a$$, **not** which $$\alpha$$

***

## C4) Projectors

### Non‑degenerate projector

$$
P_a = |a\rangle\langle a|
$$

### Degenerate projector

$$
P_a = \sum_{\alpha=1}^g |a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha|
$$

### Properties

$$
P_a^2 = P_a,\quad P_a^\dagger = P_a
$$

$$
P_a P_b = 0 \quad (a\neq b)
$$

***

## C5) Spectral decomposition (discrete)

For a Hermitian operator:

$$
\boxed{\hat A = \sum_a a\,P_a}
$$

This is the **operator form** of “diagonalization”.

Matrix form:

$$
A = \sum_a a \sum_{\alpha}|a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha|
$$

***

## C6) Probabilities

If the system is in state $$|\psi\rangle$$, probability of outcome $$a$$ is:

$$
P(a)=\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle
$$

***

## C7) Lüders rule (state update)

### Non‑degenerate

$$
|\psi\rangle \;\longrightarrow\;
\frac{P_a|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}}
$$

### Degenerate (Lüders rule)

$$
\boxed{
|\psi\rangle \;\longrightarrow\;
\frac{P_a|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}}
\quad\text{with } P_a=\sum_\alpha |a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha|
}
$$

**Key idea:**

*   measurement collapses **onto the eigenspace**, not a particular eigenvector

***

## C8) Synthesis (Sakurai ↔ Dirac ↔ Matrix)

| Measurement (Sakurai) | Abstract (Dirac) | Matrix                |
| --------------------- | ---------------- | --------------------- |
| outcome $$a$$         | eigenvalue       | eigenvalue            |
| compatible state      | eigenstate       | eigenvector           |
| degeneracy            | subspace         | eigenspace            |
| repeatability         | projection       | idempotence           |
| state update          | Lüders rule      | normalized projection |

## **Packet L04‑S4 — Degenerate projector**

```latex
\section*{L04-S4 Degenerate projector — Problem}
Let $\hat A$ have eigenvalue $a$ with orthonormal eigenstates $\{|a,1\rangle,|a,2\rangle\}$.
Construct the projector $P_a$ and show it is Hermitian and idempotent.

\newpage
\section*{Suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Sum dyads $|a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha|$.
\item Use orthonormality.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Advanced suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Check $P_a^2$ term by term.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Detailed solution}
\[
P_a = |a,1\rangle\langle a,1| + |a,2\rangle\langle a,2|.
\]
Hermiticity is immediate. For idempotence:
\[
P_a^2 = \sum_{\alpha,\beta}|a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha|a,\beta\rangle\langle a,\beta|
= \sum_{\alpha}|a,\alpha\rangle\langle a,\alpha| = P_a.
\]
```

## **Packet L04‑S8 — Lüders rule (degenerate)**

```latex
\section*{L04-S8 Lüders rule — Problem}
Let $P_a$ be the projector onto a degenerate eigenspace of $\hat A$.
Show that the post‑measurement state
\[
|\psi'\rangle=\frac{P_a|\psi\rangle}{\sqrt{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}}
\]
is normalized.

\newpage
\section*{Suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Compute $\langle\psi'|\psi'\rangle$.
\item Use $P_a^2=P_a$.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Advanced suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Insert $P_a^\dagger=P_a$.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Detailed solution}
\[
\langle\psi'|\psi'\rangle
=\frac{\langle\psi|P_a P_a|\psi\rangle}{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}
=\frac{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}{\langle\psi|P_a|\psi\rangle}=1.
\]
```

***

## **Packet L04‑C4 — Lüders vs naive collapse**

```latex
\section*{L04-C4 Lüders vs naive — Problem}
Compare Lüders rule with a naive ``collapse to one eigenvector'' rule for a degenerate measurement.
Explain why the naive rule is physically incorrect.

\newpage
\section*{Suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Ask: what information does the measurement provide?
\item Consider repeatability.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Advanced suggestions}
\begin{itemize}
\item Show naive collapse introduces extra information not measured.
\end{itemize}

\newpage
\section*{Detailed solution}
A degenerate measurement determines only the eigenvalue $a$, not the label $\alpha$.
Collapsing to a specific $|a,\alpha\rangle$ would add information not present in the measurement outcome.
Lüders rule projects onto the full eigenspace, preserving exactly the information gained and no more.
```
