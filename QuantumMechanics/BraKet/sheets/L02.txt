# C) Lecture notes (Narrative + derivations)

## C1) Sakurai track — transition amplitudes

Operationally, a transition amplitude compares two preparations:

$$
\text{prepare }|\psi\rangle \quad\text{measure outcome }|a\rangle.
$$

The complex number $$\langle a|\psi\rangle$$ encodes the probability via

$$
P(a\leftarrow\psi)=|\langle a|\psi\rangle|^2.
$$

This forces a structure:

*   linearity in the state being prepared
*   complex conjugation when reversing roles (prepare ↔ test)

***

## C2) Dirac track — bras as dual vectors

### Definition (physics convention)

For every ket $$|\psi\rangle$$, there is a bra $$\langle\psi|$$ such that

$$
\langle\psi|\phi\rangle \in \mathbb C
$$

is the inner product.

### Linearity rules

*   Linear in the **ket**:

$$
\langle\phi|(a|\psi_1\rangle+b|\psi_2\rangle)
= a\langle\phi|\psi_1\rangle + b\langle\phi|\psi_2\rangle
$$

*   **Antilinear** in the bra:

$$
(a\langle\phi_1|+b\langle\phi_2|)|\psi\rangle
= a^*\langle\phi_1|\psi\rangle + b^*\langle\phi_2|\psi\rangle
$$

This is why complex conjugation appears.

***

## C3) Inner product axioms

For all $$|\phi\rangle,|\psi\rangle$$:

1.  Conjugate symmetry

$$
\langle\phi|\psi\rangle=\langle\psi|\phi\rangle^*
$$

2.  Linearity in the ket

3.  Positivity

$$
\langle\psi|\psi\rangle \ge 0,\quad =0 \iff |\psi\rangle=0
$$

Define norm:

$$
\|\psi\|=\sqrt{\langle\psi|\psi\rangle}
$$

***

## C4) Geometry and overlaps

For normalized states,

$$
|\langle\phi|\psi\rangle| \le 1
$$

(guaranteed by Cauchy–Schwarz).

*   $$|\langle\phi|\psi\rangle|=1$$: same ray
*   $$|\langle\phi|\psi\rangle|=0$$: orthogonal (mutually exclusive outcomes)

## **Packet L02‑S2 — Normalization via inner product**

### Problem

Normalize $$|\psi\rangle = (1+i)|1\rangle + 2|2\rangle$$.

### Suggestions

*   Compute $$\langle\psi|\psi\rangle$$.
*   Divide by $$\sqrt{\langle\psi|\psi\rangle}$$.

### Advanced suggestions

*   Track complex conjugation carefully.
*   Interpret normalization probabilistically.

### Detailed solution

$$
\langle\psi|\psi\rangle
=(1-i)(1+i)\langle1|1\rangle +4\langle2|2\rangle
=2+4=6.
$$

Thus

$$
|\psi\rangle_{\text{norm}}=\frac{1}{\sqrt6}\big((1+i)|1\rangle+2|2\rangle\big).
$$

## **Packet L02‑S6 — Bra–ket ↔ matrix translation**

### Problem

Let

$$
|\psi\rangle=\begin{pmatrix}1\\ i\end{pmatrix}.
$$

Write $$\langle\psi|$$ and compute $$\langle\psi|\psi\rangle$$.

### Suggestions

*   Take conjugate transpose.

### Advanced suggestions

*   Compare with Euclidean dot product.

### Detailed solution

$$
\langle\psi|=(1,-i),\quad
\langle\psi|\psi\rangle=1+1=2.
$$
