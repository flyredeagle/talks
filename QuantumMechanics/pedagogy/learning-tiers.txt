# Learning Tiers (HS ‚Üí PhD)

## **Tier 1 ‚Äî High School (HS) prerequisites**

**Goal:** Build the *intuition tools* needed for amplitudes and interference.  
**Core competencies:**

*   Complex numbers as ‚Äú2D arrows‚Äù: $$a+ib$$, magnitude $$|z|$$, phase $$\arg z$$
*   **Magnitude squared** as probability: $$P \propto |A|^2$$
*   **Vector addition analogy**: adding complex amplitudes before squaring ‚Üí interference
*   Basic probability: events, normalization (‚Äútotal probability = 1‚Äù)

**You should be able to:**

*   Compute $$|a+ib|^2$$
*   Understand why $$|A_1+A_2|^2\neq |A_1|^2+|A_2|^2$$ in general

***

## **Tier 2 ‚Äî Beginning Undergraduate (BegUG)**

**Goal:** Become fluent in the *working language* of bras, kets, bases, and probabilities.  
**Core competencies:**

*   Normalization: $$\langle \psi|\psi\rangle=1$$
*   Orthonormal bases: $$\langle m|n\rangle=\delta_{mn}$$
*   Components as inner products:
    $$
    c_n=\langle n|\psi\rangle
    $$
*   Probabilities:
    $$
    P(n)=|c_n|^2
    $$
*   ‚ÄúInsert identity‚Äù in discrete form:
    $$
    \sum_n |n\rangle\langle n|=\mathbb I
    $$

**You should be able to:**

*   Expand a state in a basis and compute probabilities
*   Translate between $$|\psi\rangle$$ and its coordinate column vector in a chosen basis

***

## **Tier 3 ‚Äî Advanced Undergraduate (AdvUG)**

**Goal:** Understand why the formalism is *internally consistent* and geometric.  
**Core competencies:**

*   **Cauchy‚ÄìSchwarz inequality**:
    $$
    |\langle\phi|\psi\rangle|^2\le \langle\phi|\phi\rangle\langle\psi|\psi\rangle
    $$
*   **Triangle inequality** for norms:
    $$
    \|\phi+\psi\|\le \|\phi\|+\|\psi\|
    $$
*   Metric/geometric viewpoint:
    *   overlaps behave like cosines of angles (up to phase)
    *   distance notions emerge from $$\|\phi-\psi\|$$ (with caveats about phase)

**You should be able to:**

*   Prove basic inequalities in Hilbert space
*   Explain why overlaps and probabilities are bounded by 1 for normalized states
*   See superposition as linear structure, not ‚Äúmystery‚Äù

***

## **Tier 4 ‚Äî Master‚Äôs (MSc)**

**Goal:** Shift from ‚Äúvectors as states‚Äù to ‚Äúrays as states‚Äù and connect to symmetry theory.  
**Core competencies:**

*   **Rays / projective Hilbert space** viewpoint:
    *   physical states are equivalence classes $$ |\psi\rangle\sim e^{i\theta}|\psi\rangle$$
    *   mathematically, pure states are points in **projective space** $$\mathbb P(\mathcal H)$$
*   **Wigner theorem preview**:
    *   transformations preserving transition probabilities
        $$|\langle\phi|\psi\rangle|^2$$
        are implemented by **unitary or antiunitary** operators

**You should be able to:**

*   Explain why global phase is unphysical and why rays are the correct objects
*   State what Wigner‚Äôs theorem says and why it matters for symmetries

***

## **Tier 5 ‚Äî PhD**

**Goal:** Make all foundational statements **mathematically precise** and know where physics shorthand hides subtleties.  
**Core competencies:**

*   Precise definition of **Hilbert space** (inner product + completeness)
*   **Separability** (existence of a countable orthonormal basis / countable dense subset) and why it‚Äôs assumed in most QM
*   Rays as **1D subspaces** (not just ‚Äúvectors up to phase‚Äù)
*   Awareness of operator subtleties:
    *   Hermitian vs self-adjoint (domains matter)
    *   generalized eigenvectors and distributional states (preview for rigged Hilbert spaces)

**You should be able to:**

*   Distinguish informal bra‚Äìket manipulations from rigorous operator statements
*   Discuss why separability matters for spectral decompositions and expansions
*   Frame state space as a projective object and symmetry as structure on that space

***

# One-line summary of the ladder

*   **HS:** amplitudes are complex arrows ‚Üí probabilities are squared lengths
*   **BegUG:** bases/components ‚Üí compute probabilities with $$\langle n|\psi\rangle$$
*   **AdvUG:** inequalities ‚Üí geometry and consistency of the formalism
*   **MSc:** rays/projective space ‚Üí symmetries (Wigner theorem)
*   **PhD:** rigorous Hilbert space + separability + domain subtleties
