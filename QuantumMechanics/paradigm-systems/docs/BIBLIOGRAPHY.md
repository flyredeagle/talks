# Tiered Bibliography — Module I.4: Paradigm Physical Systems

Rating: ★★★ essential · ★★ recommended · ★ supplementary  
Tags: [N] narrative/popular · [T] textbook · [M] monograph · [P] primary paper · [R] review

---

## Tier 1 — High School (Popular / Narrative)

**[A1] ★★★ [N]**  
Feynman, R.P., *QED: The Strange Theory of Light and Matter*, Princeton UP, 1985.  
[T1] — Physical intuition for quantum amplitude and probability; suitable prelude to L01 and L04.

**[A2] ★★ [N]**  
Cox, B. & Forshaw, J., *The Quantum Universe*, Da Capo Press, 2011.  
[T1] — Accessible treatment of wave–particle duality, energy levels, and the hydrogen atom (L01, L09).

**[A3] ★ [N]**  
Gamow, G., *Thirty Years That Shook Physics*, Dover, 1966.  
[T1] — Historical narrative; strong on Bohr model, alpha decay, and the Zeeman effect (L06, L12).

---

## Tier 2 — Beginning Undergraduate Textbooks

**[B1] ★★★ [T]**  
Griffiths, D.J., *Introduction to Quantum Mechanics*, 3rd ed., Cambridge UP, 2018.  
[T2][T3] — Primary text for L01–L12. Ch. 2 (free particle, box, HO), Ch. 4 (hydrogen atom, Zeeman),  
Ch. 5 (identical particles), Ch. 6 (perturbation theory). Directly supports SPSU problems throughout.

**[B2] ★★★ [T]**  
Atkins, P.W. & Friedman, R., *Molecular Quantum Mechanics*, 5th ed., Oxford UP, 2011.  
[T2][T3] — Good coverage of hydrogen, Zeeman, Stark, and Rabi model (L04, L06, L07, L09).  
Useful for SPSU Part B interpretation questions.

**[B3] ★★ [T]**  
Gasiorowicz, S., *Quantum Physics*, 3rd ed., Wiley, 2003.  
[T2][T3] — Clear treatment of barrier tunnelling (L10–L11); strong worked examples for SPSU.

## Tier 3 — Advanced Undergraduate (Bridge Texts)

**[B4] ★★★ [T]**  
Shankar, R., *Principles of Quantum Mechanics*, 2nd ed., Springer, 1994.  
[T3] — Chapter 7 (harmonic oscillator), Chapter 12 (hydrogen). Excellent for CPSU derivations.  
Highly recommended alongside Griffiths from L03 onward.

**[B5] ★★ [T]**  
Landau, L.D. & Lifshitz, E.M., *Quantum Mechanics (Non-Relativistic Theory)*, 3rd ed., Pergamon, 1977.  
[T3][T4] — §23 (delta potential), §50 (WKB), §74 (Stark), §113 (Zeeman). Dense but authoritative.  
Essential for CPSU L07, L12.

**[B6] ★★ [P]** — **Tier 3 primary paper for L02:**  
Bloch, F., "Über die Quantenmechanik der Elektronen in Kristallgittern," *Z. Phys.* 52 (1928) 555–600.  
[T3][T4] — Original derivation of Bloch's theorem. Connects to CPSU L02, WRQ L02.

## Tier 4 — Graduate Textbooks

**[C1] ★★★ [T]**  
Sakurai, J.J. & Napolitano, J., *Modern Quantum Mechanics*, 3rd ed., Cambridge UP, 2021.  
[T4] — Ch. 2 (harmonic oscillator, hydrogen), Ch. 4 (perturbation theory: Zeeman/Stark),  
Ch. 5 (symmetries). The programme's primary graduate reference. SCQG/CPSG throughout.

**[C2] ★★★ [T]**  
Cohen-Tannoudji, C., Diu, B. & Laloë, F., *Quantum Mechanics*, Vols. I–II, Wiley, 1977.  
[T4] — Vol. II: spin–orbit (L08), Stark (L07), Zeeman (L06), scattering (L10–L11).  
Complement sections invaluable for SPJG projects.

**[C3] ★★ [T]**  
Allen, L. & Eberly, J.H., *Optical Resonance and Two-Level Atoms*, Dover, 1987.  
[T4][T5] — Definitive graduate treatment of the Rabi model (L04). Required for CPSG L04,  
CPJG L04, and WRQ/ORQ on quantum control.

**[C4] ★★★ [T]**  
Ashcroft, N.W. & Mermin, N.D., *Solid State Physics*, HRW, 1976.  
[T4] — Ch. 2 (free electron model, DOS), Ch. 8–9 (Bloch/nearly-free electron, L02).  
Essential for WRQ L01–L02 on condensed matter applications.

**[C5] ★★ [T]**  
Weinberg, S., *Lectures on Quantum Mechanics*, 2nd ed., Cambridge UP, 2015.  
[T4][T5] — Fine structure (L08), symmetry and conservation laws (L05–L06).  
Good complement to Sakurai for CCQG questions.

**[C6] ★★ [P]** — **Tier 4 primary paper for L03:**  
Glauber, R.J., "Coherent and Incoherent States of the Radiation Field," *Phys. Rev.* 131 (1963) 2766.  
[T4][T5] — Original definition and properties of coherent states. Links to CPSG L03, WRQ L03.

**[C7] ★★ [P]** — **Tier 4 primary paper for L05:**  
Aharonov, Y. & Bohm, D., "Significance of Electromagnetic Potentials in the Quantum Theory,"  
*Phys. Rev.* 115 (1959) 485.  
[T4][T5] — Original Aharonov–Bohm paper. Links to CPSG L05, ORQ L05.

**[C8] ★★ [P]** — **Tier 4 primary paper for L12:**  
Gamow, G., "Zur Quantentheorie des Atomkernes," *Z. Phys.* 51 (1928) 204.  
[T3][T4] — Original derivation of alpha-decay tunnelling rate. Links to CPSU L12, WRQ L12.

## Tier 5 — Research Monographs and PhD Papers

**[D1] ★★★ [M]**  
Reed, M. & Simon, B., *Methods of Mathematical Physics*, Vol. I–IV, Academic Press, 1972–1979.  
[T5] — Vol. II (self-adjoint extensions, spectral theory), Vol. IV (scattering theory).  
Required for CPJG L01 (spectral zeta), CPJG L10–L11 (S-matrix rigour).

**[D2] ★★ [M]**  
Galindo, A. & Pascual, P., *Quantum Mechanics*, Vols. I–II, Springer, 1990.  
[T5] — Rigorous treatment of the hydrogen atom (SO(4) symmetry, L09), WKB (L12).  
Links to CPJG L09 (Runge–Lenz algebra).

**[D3] ★★ [M]**  
Thirring, W., *A Course in Mathematical Physics, Vol. 3: Quantum Mechanics of Atoms and Molecules*,  
Springer, 1981.  
[T5] — Rigorous spectral theory of hydrogen. Connects to ORQ L09.

**[D4] ★★ [M]**  
Berestetskii, V.B., Lifshitz, E.M. & Pitaevskii, L.P., *Quantum Electrodynamics*,  
Pergamon, 1982.  
[T5] — Relativistic corrections, fine structure from Dirac equation (L08). Links to CPJG L08.

**[D5] ★★ [M]**  
Büttiker, M. & Landauer, R., "Traversal Time for Tunneling," *Phys. Rev. Lett.* 49 (1982) 1739.  
[T5] — Tunnelling time and Hartman effect. Links to ORQ L10.
